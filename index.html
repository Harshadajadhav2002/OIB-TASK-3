Major Prajakta Desai

Early Life

Major Prajakta Desai was born on October 22, 1987, in Mumbai, India. She grew up in a middle-class family and attended school in Mumbai. After completing her schooling, she joined the National Defence Academy (NDA) in Pune, Maharashtra, in 2004.

Military Career

After completing her training at NDA, Major Prajakta Desai was commissioned into the Corps of Signals of the Indian Army as a Lieutenant in 2008. She then went on to complete her post-graduation in Computer Science from Pune University. She was later posted to Jammu and Kashmir where she served in the 21st Signal Battalion of the Indian Army.

During her service, Major Desai was involved in several counter-insurgency operations in Jammu and Kashmir. She was also part of a team that was responsible for establishing and maintaining communication lines in the region. Her excellent leadership skills and technical knowledge helped her to excel in her role and she was promoted to the rank of Major in 2018.

Achievements

  • Received the Sena Medal for her role in the 2016 surgical strikes across the Line of Control (LoC).
  • Played a key role in establishing communication lines in the aftermath of the 2019 Pulwama attack.
  • Has been awarded the Chief of Army Staff's (COAS) Commendation Card twice for her outstanding service.
